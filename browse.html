Currently IBM has a leading program based on the development of star polymer nanoparticles for biomedical applications, including advances in polymer synthesis, nanoparticle characterization, and a new strategic partnership with UC Berkeley. Recently IBM has developed the first use of non-metal organocatalysis to create complex unimolecular organic architectures that are the first materials in their class to load and release hydrophobic cargo under physiological conditions. What is needed now is a full and detailed understanding of the way in which these complex molecular architectures exist in an aqueous environment. This project involves primarily a combination of advanced molecular simulation of star polymer properties and analysis of recently obtained small angle X-ray scattering of star polymers. This is part of the QUEST program

Help design and build a 3d modeling environment for architects. http://decod.es/ is a a platform-agnostic geometry library, a common development environment, and a collaborative community that promotes computational literacy in architectural design. decod.es is free, open-source, and built to seamlessly interface with a number of proprietary CAD packages, thereby opening computational design to students and independent designers who would otherwise be unable to afford expensive software licenses. Inspired by the Processing development environment, a programming community founded in 2001, this project seeks to transfer the gains achieved in the visual arts through the introduction of easily accessible computing to the unique needs of architects. By providing free and open access to computational design techniques, the Decodes project seeks to promote computational literacy in architects - a prerequisite for collaboration with a number of disciplines that are increasingly quantitative computationally adept, and essential to a contemporary and responsible practice of architecture.

Research Assistant: Big Data stream processing

This research project explores the hottest area in Big Data: scalable real-time stream processing. We need to evaluate several emerging frameworks (Storm, SparkStreaming, AWS Kinesis), particularly with respect to suitability for processing multimedia streams such as television channels and photo/video-sharing via social media. The objective for this summer is to demonstrate a prototype that can filter massive amounts of real-time streaming data according to specified trigger keywords; for now it can be text-based, but ultimately it will integrate speech and image analysis. This work will be performed at two sites in downtown Berkeley: the International Computer Science Institute and the SkyDeck startup accelerator.

Implementing and evaluating network discovery policies in Android.

This is an opportunity to join a team that's building a tool which enables people to communicate in the face of country-wide Internet blackouts. The tool runs on mobile phones which form an ad-hoc network, exchanging messages when two phones come close enough to communicate directly over wifi. We're looking for a programmer comfortable with Java and Android development who will implement and evaluate several approaches to the way that the phones decide when and whether to communicate with other phones given privacy and battery constraints. This will involve reading and understanding algorithms in scientific papers and then implementing them in code. You'll be working closely with up to 4 other programmers who are building other aspects of the app, but you'll need to be comfortable moving ahead toward milestones on your own. We'll design the milestones together and expect you to come to our meetings with concrete designs, graphs or code that we can look at and work from, even when they're not perfect. Project: Rangzen Organization: De Novo Group - a nonprofit corporation - www.denovogroup,org and the TIER research group: http:///tier.cs.berkeley.edu/
